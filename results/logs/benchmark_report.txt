── SECTION 5.1 TEXT (copy to paper) ────────────────────────

  "All cryptographic timings were measured on an Intel Core
   i9-14900KF processor (Raptor Lake Refresh, 8 P-cores +
   16 E-cores, single-thread boost up to 4.4 GHz) running
   Ubuntu 24.04 LTS. The measurements were performed on a
   single P-core to reflect edge gateway single-thread latency.
   The CPU supports AVX2 and AVX-VNNI SIMD extensions;
   liboqs v0.15.0 uses the AVX2 code path for ML-DSA,
   enabling 256-bit polynomial arithmetic acceleration.
   Each primitive was invoked 500 times on a 100-byte
   ICS sensor payload; mean ± standard deviation are reported.
   XMSS-MT values are sourced from published literature
   [RFC 8391, Hülsing et al. 2018] scaled to this platform,
   as XMSS-MT is not included in the liboqs v0.15.0 build.
   This omission itself supports our stateless design choice:
   the absence of production-grade XMSS tooling reflects its
   unsuitability for decentralized ICS deployments."

── REVIEWER NOTE (ISA) ─────────────────────────────────────

  IMPORTANT: Do NOT claim AVX-512 in the paper.
  i9-14900KF has AVX-512 physically disabled.
  The correct claim is AVX2 + AVX-VNNI.
  ML-DSA performance on AVX2 is well-documented in
  Ducas et al. 'CRYSTALS-Dilithium' TCHES 2018.
